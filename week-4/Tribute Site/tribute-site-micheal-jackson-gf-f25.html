Micheal Jackson Tribute

Micheal Jackson

A global superstar, “king of pop”, and humanitarian activist.

Micheal's Legacy

  • 1964-1978 : Early Career- Micheal started his career in 1964 alongside his four brothers as part of
    the group The Jackson 5. The brothers performed and released several studio albums
    from up until 1989.
  • 1979-1984 : Transition to Solo Career- In October of 1979, Micheal released his first independent
    album titled Off the Wall. By 1984, Micheal parted ways with the Jackson 5 in favor of
    focusing on his path as a solo artist, liberating him from the pressure of the group.
  • 1985-1993 : Early Activism- In 1985, Micheal helped write “We Are the World”, a charity record
    that helped raise around $63 million for famine relief and humanitarian aid. Later in
    1992, Jackson established the Heal the World Foundation to aid children in neglect.
  • 1994-2001 : Continued Career- By the time he released his last album Invincible in 2001, Micheal
    had received a total of 13 Grammy Awards and published a total of ten studio albums
    totaling to 1 billion records sold. Around $300 million of such revenue went to charity.
  • 2002-2009 : End of Life- By the end of his life, Micheal had fathered three children and amassed a
    large estate under his name. In June of 2009, Micheal sadly passed of an overdose on
    propofol, an anesthetic being administered to him for personal medical care.
